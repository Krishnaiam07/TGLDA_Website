Role and Responsibilities of Gopalamithras / Pashu Mithras / MAITRIs

User Manual
Artificial Insemination (AI), Pregnancy Diagnosis (PD) & Calving

Gopalamithras, Pashu Mithras, and MAITRIs are the primary field-level service providers of the Telangana Livestock Development Agency (TGLDA), working directly with livestock farmers to strengthen cattle and buffalo breeding services across the State. They serve as an important link between farmers, Veterinary Assistant Surgeons (VASs), Supervisors, and District Livestock Development Agencies (DLDAs), ensuring that quality breeding services are delivered efficiently at the farmer's doorstep. Their responsibilities include identifying eligible animals for breeding, educating livestock owners on scientific animal husbandry practices, promoting timely artificial insemination services, maintaining accurate breeding records, and assisting in the successful implementation of various livestock development programmes undertaken by TGLDA.

In addition to breeding services, Gopalamithras, Pashu Mithras, and MAITRIs play a significant role in creating awareness among farmers regarding balanced feeding, clean animal housing, disease prevention, vaccination schedules, deworming programmes, calf management, and improved livestock management practices. They encourage farmers to adopt scientific breeding technologies, provide guidance on the proper care of breeding animals, assist in identifying reproductive disorders, and support government initiatives aimed at improving livestock productivity. They also coordinate closely with Veterinary Officers, Supervisors, and DLDA officials by submitting field reports, maintaining official records, supporting monitoring activities, and ensuring that all breeding programmes are implemented effectively. Through their dedicated field services and continuous interaction with livestock owners, they contribute significantly to genetic improvement, increased milk production, sustainable livestock development, and the economic well-being of farming families across Telangana.

Process of doing Artificial Insemination (AI), Pregnancy Diagnosis (PD) & Calf

Artificial Insemination (AI) is the primary breeding service carried out by Gopalamithras, Pashu Mithras, and MAITRIs to improve the genetic quality and productivity of cattle and buffaloes. When a farmer observes heat symptoms in an animal, such as restlessness, mounting behaviour, clear mucus discharge, reduced feed intake, or frequent bellowing, the field worker is immediately informed. The field worker visits the farmer's location with the Artificial Insemination kit, frozen semen container, AI gun, disposable sheath, gloves, lubricant, and other required equipment. Before performing insemination, the animal is carefully examined to confirm that it is in the correct stage of heat and physically fit for breeding. The farmer is also advised about the importance of timely insemination and proper handling of the animal during the procedure.

After confirming the animal's suitability, the required frozen semen straw is safely removed from the Liquid Nitrogen (LN₂) container using approved handling techniques to maintain semen quality. The semen straw is thawed in clean water at the recommended temperature for the prescribed duration. Once thawed, the straw is dried with a clean tissue, loaded carefully into a sterilized Artificial Insemination gun, and covered with a sterile disposable sheath. Following strict hygiene and biosecurity practices, the field worker performs the insemination using the recto-vaginal technique. During this procedure, the semen is accurately deposited in the reproductive tract near the body of the uterus, ensuring maximum chances of conception while minimizing stress or injury to the animal. After completing the procedure, all used materials are disposed of safely, and the animal owner is advised to provide proper feeding, adequate water, and avoid unnecessary stress to the animal.

Immediately after the Artificial Insemination is completed, the field worker records all mandatory information in the Bharat Pashudhan Application and other prescribed registers. These records include the farmer's details, animal identification number, species, breed, village, date and time of insemination, semen straw identification number, sire details, technician details, and other breeding information required for official records. Accurate data entry is essential for monitoring conception rates, evaluating breeding performance, maintaining traceability, and ensuring transparency in livestock development programmes implemented by TGLDA.

Pregnancy Diagnosis (PD) is carried out approximately 60 to 90 days after Artificial Insemination by a qualified Veterinary Assistant Surgeon (VAS) or authorized veterinarian. During Pregnancy Diagnosis, the reproductive organs of the animal are examined to determine whether conception has occurred successfully. If the animal is confirmed pregnant, the result is recorded in the Bharat Pashudhan Application, and the farmer is advised regarding balanced nutrition, vaccination schedules, deworming, mineral supplementation, and proper pregnancy management until calving. If the animal is found to be non-pregnant, the reason is assessed wherever possible, the farmer is counselled accordingly, and the animal is monitored for the next heat cycle so that another timely insemination can be performed.

After successful completion of pregnancy, the animal eventually gives birth to a healthy calf. Following calving, the field worker visits the farmer, verifies the birth details, and records the calf information in the Bharat Pashudhan Application. The registration includes the calf's date of birth, sex, breed, identification details, dam and sire information, owner details, village, and other prescribed particulars. Proper calf registration enables the Government and TGLDA to maintain complete breeding records, monitor the performance of Artificial Insemination programmes, assess genetic improvement, plan future breeding strategies, and provide eligible benefits under various livestock development schemes. The field worker also educates the farmer regarding colostrum feeding, calf care, vaccination, nutrition, and scientific management practices to ensure healthy growth and improved future productivity.
